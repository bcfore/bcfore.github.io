Web development

I've been taking online courses through Launch School for about a year now (as of July 2017), with about a 50/50 split between front-end and back-end work.

The emphasis at Launch School is on mastering the fundamentals. You can read more about their teaching philosophy here. This approach worked well for me. I want my understanding of the fundamental concepts to be strong enough that I can not only use higher-level frameworks, but implement them myself when needed.

The back-end courses focused on Ruby. I also solidified my knowledge of SQL (I already had a good grasp of SQL from my career in finance) and learned how to deploy to Heroku. The front-end courses focused on JavaScript, jQuery, and Backbone (in addition to the fundamentals of HTML and CSS).
